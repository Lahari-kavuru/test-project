Welcome to the Centennial State !!

Colorado's history is marked by the Indigenous peoples who first inhabited the region, including the Ute, Cheyenne, and Arapaho tribes.
The area came under Spanish and later Mexican rule in the 18th and early 19th centuries before becoming part of the United States with the 1848 Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo.
The Colorado Gold Rush of the 1850s brought a rapid influx of settlers, leading to statehood in 1876, making Colorado the 38th state in the Union.
The state's history is intertwined with mining, ranching, and the rugged beauty of the Rocky Mountains, and it's also known for its role in the development of environmental conservation,
particularly through the establishment of Rocky Mountain National Park in 1915.

Maroon Bells

A pair of iconic and photogenic mountain peaks in the Elk Mountains of Colorado, celebrated for their stunning reflection in Maroon Lake, offering some of the most picturesque vistas in the state.

Silverton

A historic mining town in southwestern Colorado, known for its charming Victorian architecture and its role in the region's rich mining heritage.

Royal Gorge Bridge

A world-famous suspension bridge spanning the Arkansas River in Colorado, offering breathtaking views and thrilling experiences in one of the highest suspension bridges in the world.
